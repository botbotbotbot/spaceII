11 reasons fonts are really important and I'm glad I spent 4+ years studying them

  1. It's a fun party trick! That beer logo uses Copperplate. Do YOU know which one I'm talking about? You would if you went to college specifically to study it!
  2. You can impress other humans. The other day, I guessed that a poster used Avenir. It did, obviously. The dude who made it was so impressed, that he told me his heart skipped a beat, sacrified three lizard people to try to win my love, and then followed me to train station.
  3. You can have opinions on things and use them as a way to prove your worth as a human being! What's the point of living if you don't have any strong opinions? Helvetica anyone?! What, you saw that documentary too, but obviously had opinions on that typeface beforehand, but now have opinions on it in a historical context? HILARIOUS! Let's talk about it for HOURS.
  4. You can even have opinions on other people's opinions! Did someone use the word font when they should have said typeface? What a fool, better silently judge them. Did someone just call out a poster that used comic sans? Really, you're still on that, dude?
  5. It's a fun car game! Guess the typefaces that are used on every billboard and poster that you pass. Do the same for street signs! Did you know that highway signs have their OWN CUSTOM TYPEFACE called Interstate? I DO. Good god, what an important thing to be taking up space in my brain!
  6. Lets you find fault ALMOST ANYWHERE! Sure, that typeface is legible, and makes sense in that context, but look at the kerning between that w and that r. PUH-LEEZE.
  7. Know more about ad campaigns than other people. Did you know that the Obama change campaign was part of what made the typeface Gotham so popular? Because I do! Why do I know that? Who knows!
  8. You can constantly feel bad about your own decisions. Did I really use Gotham in that document, even though it's not part of our branding, and horrifically overused since that Obama campaign? Ugh, what's wrong with me. Let's look for a bridge to jump off of before I can make a decision like that again next week.
  9. Really though, typefaces are basically garbage once they've started to be overused. That's another fun thing about typefaces, if you find one you like, knowing that you might not be able to use it at all in a few years and everything you used it in is basically toxic garbage.
  10. This is especially true for scripts. But why are you even using a script typeface in the first place you piece of human filth? Those are basically fake fonts, which everyone can obviously tell because the letters repeat. Have you jumped off that bridge yet?
  11. Not only be able to recognize typefaces, but knowing the history of how they came to be as well. The other day, someone used Bodoni Poster Italic as an example of Bodoni, even though it was created over 100 years later in 1929, and I almost lit him on fire.

I have so many more of these, but I have to figure out public transportation into the city. Interestingly enough, using public transportation is a skill that would potentially be more useful than knowing lots of information about fonts omg bye.
